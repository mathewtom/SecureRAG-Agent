ENGINEERING CONFIDENTIAL

Incident Postmortem: Payment Processing Outage — March 14, 2026

Severity: SEV-1
Duration: 2 hours 37 minutes (14:22 UTC to 17:01 UTC)
Impact: 100% of payment transactions failed in US-EAST region
Revenue Impact: Estimated $2.1M in lost transactions
Customer Impact: ~34,000 failed checkout attempts

Root Cause:
A misconfigured connection pool limit in the payment gateway caused cascading failures when traffic exceeded 12,000 TPS during a flash sale event. The circuit breaker threshold was set to 80% error rate (should have been 40%), allowing degraded requests to propagate to downstream services for 47 minutes before the on-call engineer was paged.

Timeline:
- 14:22 UTC — Traffic spike begins (flash sale launch)
- 14:28 UTC — Connection pool exhaustion on pg-east-primary
- 14:35 UTC — Error rate exceeds 80%, circuit breaker trips (too late)
- 14:38 UTC — PagerDuty alert fires to on-call (Nina Kowalski)
- 14:52 UTC — Incident commander Sarah Chen joins
- 15:15 UTC — Root cause identified (connection pool config)
- 15:30 UTC — Hot-fix deployed: pool limit increased from 100 to 500
- 16:45 UTC — Traffic rerouted through US-WEST while east recovers
- 17:01 UTC — Full service restoration confirmed

Action Items:
1. Reduce circuit breaker threshold to 40% (completed March 15)
2. Add connection pool metrics to Grafana dashboard (completed March 18)
3. Load test at 2x peak capacity before next flash sale (due April 30)
4. Implement automatic regional failover (due June 15)

Lessons Learned:
- Default connection pool config was inherited from 2019 deployment
- On-call runbook did not include regional failover procedure
- Flash sale traffic was 3x projected (marketing did not notify engineering)

# Role Definitions

This document explains what each canonical role does and what authority it
carries. Approval thresholds are codified in `approval_matrix_2026.md`; this
document gives the rationale.

## Executive

- **Chief Executive Officer (CEO).** Final authority on company strategy and
  any expenditure exceeding the CFO's discretionary cap. Required signatory
  on all M&A activity, all regulatory filings, and any settlement above $1M.
- **Chief Operating Officer (COO).** Owns cross-functional execution,
  procurement above the director level, and people-systems decisions. Acts
  for the CEO in the CEO's absence.
- **Chief Financial Officer (CFO).** Sole signing authority for routine
  vendor contracts up to the cap defined in the current approval matrix.
  Above that cap, CEO countersign is required (this changed in 2026; see
  `approval_matrix_2025.md` vs `approval_matrix_2026.md`).
- **General Counsel (GC).** Required reviewer for any contract with terms
  longer than 24 months, any agreement involving customer data processing,
  and any settlement.
- **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).** Owns the incident response
  playbook, the data classification policy, and the security review of all
  vendor onboarding above the threshold defined in `approval_matrix_2026.md`.

## VP-level

- **Vice President of Engineering / Product / Sales / Marketing / People.**
  Each VP is the budget owner for their function. They may approve
  expenditures within their function up to the limit set by the matrix
  without further escalation.

## Director-level

- **Directors** approve hiring within an approved headcount plan, expense
  reports up to the matrix limit, and contracts up to the same limit.
  Directors do not have signing authority for vendor MSAs.

## Manager-level

- **Engineering / Sales / HR / Marketing managers** are the primary approver
  for direct-report expense reports and travel pre-approval. They escalate
  to their director for anything above the matrix limit. They are NOT
  budget owners.

## Individual contributor

- **Senior / Staff ICs** may make technical decisions within their team
  scope. They have no signing authority and no approval authority for
  expense reports other than their own.

## Special roles

- **Executive Assistant to the CEO (E045).** Has scheduled access to the
  CEO's calendar including Restricted events. May NOT view content of those
  events unless individually invited.
- **Paralegal (E043).** May draft but not sign legal documents. Final
  signature requires Senior Counsel or above.

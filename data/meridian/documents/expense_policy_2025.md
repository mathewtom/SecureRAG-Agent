# Expense Policy — Effective 2025-01-01 (SUPERSEDED 2026-01-01)

This is the 2025 version, retained for historical lookup. The current
policy is `expense_policy_2026.md`. The most material 2025-to-2026 change
was the reduction of CFO discretionary signing authority from $250k to
$100k.

## Travel

- **Air travel.** Economy on flights under five hours; business class
  permitted on international travel exceeding nine hours, or with VP
  approval.
- **Lodging.** Up to $275/night in Tier 1 cities; up to $175/night
  elsewhere. (2026 raised both caps.)
- **Meals.** $65/day per diem domestically, $90/day internationally.
  (2026 raised the rates by $10.)
- **Ground transport.** Rideshare, public transit, and standard taxi
  reimbursable. Personal vehicle mileage at the federal rate.
- **Parking.** Reimbursed up to $200/month for employees whose primary
  office is San Francisco or New York. (2026 raised to $250/month.)

## Vendor and SaaS

- New vendor contracts under $25k did not require security review under
  the 2025 policy. (2026 removed the dollar threshold.)
- Spend authority followed `approval_matrix_2025.md`. CFO discretionary
  signing authority extended up to $250k WITHOUT CEO countersign.
- Annual SaaS renewals over $100k required Finance review. (2026 lowered
  to $50k.)

## Reimbursement workflow

Same as 2026: 30 days to submit, 5 business days for manager approval,
10 business days for Finance processing.

## Personal device on business travel

In 2025, roaming charges on personal cell phones WERE reimbursable up to
$50/trip. The corporate eSIM program launched in late 2025 and the
reimbursement was withdrawn for 2026.

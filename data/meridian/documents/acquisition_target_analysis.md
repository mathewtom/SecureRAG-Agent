## Target overview

The candidate is a private-company peer in an adjacent product category.
Identifying details are deliberately omitted from this internal copy and
are held in the data room operated by outside counsel.

## Strategic rationale

- Adjacency: extends Meridian's product surface into a category where
  organic build would take 18+ months.
- Customer overlap: ~20% of target's named accounts are existing
  Meridian customers, meaning expansion potential rather than
  cannibalization.
- Talent: target's engineering team is well-regarded in distributed
  systems work directly relevant to Atlas (P001) and Phoenix 2.0 (P004).

## Diligence summary (in progress)

- **Financial.** 60% complete. CFO + Director of Finance are leading.
  Preliminary view: revenue quality is solid; gross margins are within
  10% of Meridian's.
- **Legal.** 40% complete. GC's office reviewing material contracts and
  IP assignments.
- **Security.** Not yet initiated. Will run under Harbor program
  conventions once the no-shop window opens.
- **Cultural.** Two informal exec-to-exec sessions completed.

## Risks

- Concentration: target's top three customers represent 38% of revenue.
- Tech: target's data plane uses a different identity model than Atlas;
  integration would require non-trivial work.
- Retention: target's CTO is the largest single talent risk.

## Decision timeline

- Diligence completion: end of Q2 2026
- Board ratification: Q3 2026 board meeting
- Public announcement (if proceeding): no earlier than Q4 2026

# Code of Conduct

Meridian expects every employee, contractor, and board member to act with
integrity. This document is the high-level statement of expected behavior;
operational detail lives in the HR Handbook and the Acceptable Use Policy.

## Core principles

- **Respect.** Treat colleagues, customers, partners, and the public with
  professionalism. Harassment of any kind is grounds for immediate
  termination.
- **Honesty.** Do not misrepresent yourself, your role, or the company.
  Do not falsify records, expense reports, or product metrics.
- **Confidentiality.** Information you receive in the course of your job is
  governed by the data classification policy. Do not share it externally
  without authorization.
- **Conflicts of interest.** Disclose any personal financial interest in a
  vendor, customer, or competitor. Recuse yourself from decisions in which
  you have an interest.
- **Compliance.** Follow the laws of every jurisdiction in which Meridian
  operates. When in doubt, ask Legal.

## Reporting concerns

Concerns about conduct may be raised to:

- Your manager
- Your manager's manager (skip-level)
- The HR Manager (E026 Brendan Walsh) or VP of People (E005 Lisa Thompson)
- The General Counsel (E006 James Okafor) for legal or compliance matters
- The anonymous ethics hotline operated by an external vendor (details in
  the HR Handbook)

Retaliation against anyone raising a good-faith concern is itself a
violation of this code.

## Acknowledgement

Every employee acknowledges this code at hire and annually thereafter.
Acknowledgement is tracked by People Operations.

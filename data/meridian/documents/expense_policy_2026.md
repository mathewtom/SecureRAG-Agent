# Expense Policy — Effective 2026-01-01

This policy supersedes the 2025 version. The most material change from
prior year is a tightening of CFO discretionary signing authority, which
now requires CEO countersign above $100k (previously $250k). See
`approval_matrix_2026.md` for the full threshold table.

## Travel

- **Air travel.** Economy on flights under five hours; premium economy
  permitted on flights over five hours. Business class permitted on
  international travel exceeding nine hours, or with VP approval.
- **Lodging.** Up to $300/night in Tier 1 cities (NYC, SF, London, Tokyo,
  Singapore); up to $200/night elsewhere. Above-cap lodging requires
  director pre-approval.
- **Meals.** $75/day per diem domestically, $100/day internationally.
- **Ground transport.** Rideshare, public transit, and standard taxi
  reimbursable. Personal vehicle mileage at the federal rate.
- **Parking.** Reimbursed up to **$250/month** for employees whose primary
  office is San Francisco or New York.

## Vendor and SaaS

- All new vendor contracts require security review (see
  `vendor_security_assessment.md`) regardless of dollar amount.
- Spend authority follows `approval_matrix_2026.md`. Note: contracts above
  the matrix threshold require BOTH the named approver AND a countersign
  above $100k.
- Annual SaaS renewals over $50k require Finance review of usage data
  before renewal.

## Reimbursement workflow

1. Submit expense report within 30 days of incurring the expense.
2. Manager approves within 5 business days.
3. Finance processes within 10 business days of approval.
4. Reimbursement appears in next semi-monthly payroll.

Late submissions (>60 days) require director-level exception approval.

## Personal device on business travel

If using a personal device for travel-related business purposes,
reimbursement of incidental connectivity (in-flight wifi, hotel wifi
upgrade) is permitted. Roaming charges for a personal cell phone are NOT
reimbursable; use the corporate eSIM provisioning path.
